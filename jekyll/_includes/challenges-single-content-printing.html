Slice

A slicer is software that slices 3D models (e.g., STL) into G-code instructions. It determines nozzle movements, material extrusion, and speeds. Adjusting its settings affects print time, part strength, and appearance.

Print Settings

  • Layer Height: Determines the thickness of each printed layer. Thinner layers yield smoother surfaces but take longer, while thicker layers reduce print time but may show more visible lines.

  • Print Speed: Controls how fast the nozzle moves. Faster speeds reduce print time but can lower quality; slower speeds enhance detail but take longer.

  • Infill Density/Pattern: Determines how solid the part is inside. Higher infill increases strength but uses more time and material; lower infill saves on both at the cost of weaker parts.

  • Shell/Wall Thickness: Adjusts the number of perimeter lines forming the outer walls. Thicker walls increase durability but require more print time; thinner walls reduce time and material usage.

  • Support Structures: Temporary material that supports overhangs or complex geometry. Improves print success but needs additional post-processing to remove.

  • Bed Adhesion (Raft/Brim/Skirt): Methods to improve how the first layer sticks to the build plate, preventing warping or detachment. They add extra material and print time.

  • Temperature Settings (Nozzle/Bed): Controls the melting and bonding of filament layers. Proper temperatures prevent defects like warping or under-extrusion.

3D Printer Setup

  1. Transfer G-code: Load the file generated by your slicer (via SD card, USB, or Wi-Fi) to your 3D printer.

  2. Bed Adhesion: Clean the build surface, level the bed, and apply any adhesive needed for proper first-layer sticking.

  3. Preheat: Heat the nozzle and build plate to the recommended temperatures for your filament.

  4. Load Filament: Ensure the filament is inserted and purged of any previous material.

  5. Start Print: Begin your print job and monitor the first few layers to check for proper adhesion.

  6. Cool & Remove: Allow the part to cool, then gently remove it from the build surface to avoid damage.

  7. Post-Process: Remove supports, sand, or paint as needed for a finished look or functionality.
